🌙 Client Report

For my site plan, I decided what content and images to use based on the purpose of my website and the message I wanted to share. I wanted my website to represent my personality, interests, and creativity, so I chose content that reflected my journey as a software engineering student, builder, and explorer. The images and design ideas were chosen to match the theme and feeling I wanted visitors to experience.

I communicated with my developer, Kyler James, through messages. We exchanged our site plans, discussed our ideas, and explained what we wanted our websites to include. We did not have any major communication issues, and we were able to complete our assignments by staying in contact and understanding each other's expectations.

My developer met my expectations for the site. The website followed the ideas from my original plan and included the important content and structure I wanted. The final result represented the vision I had when creating my site plan.

Next time I hire a team for a project, I would provide even more detailed instructions and communicate more often throughout the development process. This would help make sure every small detail matches my original idea.

🌌 General Experience and Reflections

The most challenging part of this project was making sure the final website matched the original ideas while also working with another person's expectations. I was able to overcome this by communicating with my partner and making adjustments throughout the process.

The part I enjoyed most about this project was experiencing both sides of web development. Being both a client and a developer helped me understand how important planning, communication, and creativity are when building websites.

If I could do this project again, I would spend more time planning every detail before starting development. I would also communicate more frequently during the building process to make improvements earlier.

After completing this project, I feel more confident working on real-world projects. This experience showed me that web development is not only about writing code, but also about understanding people, solving problems, and creating something meaningful for users.
